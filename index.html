Working With Local Experts

Every itinerary is built alongside people who know the ground better than any guidebook — village elders in the northern highlands, boat families on the Mekong, artisans in Hoi An's workshops, and national park rangers in Cat Tien. Working directly with local experts means guests get access that can't be booked online, and it means tourism income reaches the communities hosting the trip, not just the hotels and coach operators. This is a standing commitment across every region we operate in, not a one-off add-on.

Tour 1

Vietnam Grand Journey

The Classic Route, North to South — 5-Star Collection

Duration14 Days / 13 Nights
RouteHanoi → Halong Bay → Ninh Binh → Hue → Da Nang/Hoi An → Saigon → Mekong Delta
PaceRelaxed — classic sightseeing, private guided
Accommodation5-star hotels & luxury cruise throughout
DayItineraryBrief Introduction & ExplanationPhoto
D1Arrive HanoiPrivate transfer into the Old Quarter as dusk settles over Hoan Kiem Lake. A welcome dinner introduces the quiet rhythm of the capital before the journey south begins.
D2Hanoi City in FullTemple of Literature, the French Quarter, and the ceremony of egg coffee poured slow. Vietnam's thousand-year capital, read at walking pace.
D3Halong Bay – Embark Overnight CruiseLimestone karsts rise straight from the water as a private cruise casts off. Evening squid-fishing on deck, the bay gone silver under the moon.
D4Halong Bay – Cruise & DisembarkSunrise tai chi on the sundeck, a cave visit by rowboat, then back to shore and on toward the limestone valleys of Ninh Binh.
D5Ninh Binh – Trang An & Mua CaveA sampan drifts through flooded caves beneath towering karst — Halong Bay's inland twin. Those who climb Mua Cave's steps are rewarded with the valley laid out below.
D6Fly to Hue – Imperial CitadelThe old royal capital, walled and moated, its Forbidden Purple City still holding the hush of dynasties past.
D7Hue to Hoi An via Hai Van PassThe coastal road climbs into cloud at Hai Van Pass — one of the great drives of Vietnam — before the Golden Bridge's stone hands at Ba Na Hills and on to Hoi An.
D8Hoi An Ancient TownLantern-lit lanes, tailor ateliers, and a town that has traded quietly with the world since the 15th century.
D9Coconut Village – Fly to SaigonA basket-boat spin through the palm-lined waterways of Cam Thanh, then south by air into the neon hum of Ho Chi Minh City.
D10Saigon City TourNotre-Dame Cathedral, the Reunification Palace, and Ben Thanh Market's tangle of scent and colour — old Saigon and new, side by side.
D11Cu Chi Tunnels & Saigon by NightA morning underground in the wartime tunnel network, an evening above ground for a street-food crawl through Saigon's night markets.
D12Mekong Delta Day TripFloating markets, fruit orchards, and villages that live by the tide — a full day on the water in Vietnam's rice basket.
D13Free Day & Farewell DinnerA day left open — rooftop bar, spa, or one last wander — closing with a farewell dinner over the Saigon River skyline.
D14DepartureFinal souvenirs at Ben Thanh Market, then private transfer to the airport.
Tour 2

Vietnam Panorama

Classic Route + Trekking & Nature, North to South

Duration14 Days / 13 Nights
RouteHanoi → Sapa → Halong Bay → Ninh Binh → Hue → Hoi An → Saigon → Cat Tien
PaceActive — trekking days combined with classic sightseeing
Accommodation4–5-star hotels, mountain lodges & luxury cruise
DayItineraryBrief Introduction & ExplanationPhoto
D1Arrive HanoiThe Old Quarter's motorbike hum and lakeside calm — first steps into Vietnam before the mountains call.
D2Hanoi City TourThe Opera House, French colonial streets, and a city that wears its history in layers.
D3Overnight Train to SapaAn evening sleeper train climbs north into the mountains, cabins rocking gently toward Lao Cai.
D4Sapa Trek – Cat Cat & Muong Hoa ValleyTerraced rice fields cut into near-vertical hillsides, H'mong villages along the trail, the Hoang Lien Son range on the horizon.
D5Sapa Trek – Ta Van & Giang Ta ChaiDeeper into the valley on foot, past waterwheels and homestays, ending with tea in a hillside village.
D6Return to Hanoi – Halong Bay CruiseBack down from the mountains and straight out to sea — a private cruise casts off into Halong Bay's karst maze by afternoon.
D7Halong Bay Kayak & Ninh BinhMorning kayak through hidden lagoons, then transfer inland to the limestone valleys of Ninh Binh.
D8Ninh Binh – Trang An, Mua Cave & CyclingA sampan through flooded caves, a climb to Mua Cave's viewpoint, and an easy bike ride through rice-paddy villages.
D9Fly to Hue – Imperial CitadelThe walled royal city and a dragon boat drift on the Perfume River at day's end.
D10Hue to Hoi An via Hai Van Pass (Cycling Option)The coast road over Hai Van Pass, cycling optional on the descent, then on to Hoi An's lantern light.
D11Hoi An Ancient Town & Cham IslandsA boat out to the Cham Islands for snorkeling and quiet beaches, or a slower day exploring Hoi An's tailor shops and temples.
D12Fly to SaigonThe Saigon River lit up at dusk — the south's fast pulse after the mountains and coast.
D13Cat Tien National Park – Jungle TrekA day trek through primary rainforest, tracking gibbons and hornbills with a park naturalist.
D14DepartureA final coffee near the Central Post Office before the airport transfer.
